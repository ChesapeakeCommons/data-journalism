    Central Penn Line North and South

    The proposed Central Penn Line North and Central Penn Line South would stretch from Susquehanna to Lancaster County, Pennsylvania totaling 183 miles. Along the 125 foot right of way (ROW), it is estimated that the construction path would cross a total of 52 areas designated as “protected lands.” The northern third of the proposed path consists of intersections with state lands and one federally protected land respectively.

    North Branch Land Trust, Private Easement

    The following easement is managed by the North Branch Land Trust. The total acreage of the parcel is 94.6 acres. ASP’s proposed right of way would intersect approximately 2.5 acres of this private land.

    Mekeel and Wallace Tracts

    The following land acquisition is managed by an unspecified non-governmental organization. The total acreage of the parcel is 131.8 acres. ASP’s proposed right of way would intersect approximately 1.35 acres of this protected land.
